East Texas A&M University officially opened The Spur Expo on March 3, 2026, drawing approximately 3,000 people to the grand opening of the region's premier agricultural education facility. The well-attended event brought together local, state, and Texas A&M University System representatives alongside strong community support, showcasing the significance of the $45 million, 190,000-square-foot Agricultural Multipurpose Education and Training Center located on State Highway 24, just south of the university's main campus in Commerce.

## A Landmark Investment in Agriculture and Education

The ribbon cutting ceremony began at 5:30 p.m. with remarks from Texas A&M University System Chancellor Glenn Hegar, Vice Chancellor and CFO Ryan Griffin, and a roster of state and local officials. Following the ceremony, the East Texas A&M Lion Rodeo Team gave a demonstration at 6:30 p.m., thrilling the crowd with a preview of what the arena was built for.

> "The Spur Expo represents a strong investment in students, agriculture, and the future of rural Texas." — Chancellor Glenn Hegar

The ribbon cutting was followed by a free concert headlined by rising Texas country music star **Braxton Keith**, who took the stage at 7:30 p.m. and performed for the packed house of nearly 3,000 fans. Keith, whose single "Cozy" earned RIAA Gold certification in October 2025, is also known for hits including "I Own This Bar" and "The Chair."

East Texas A&M President Dr. Mark Rudin, who served as emcee for the evening, emphasized the facility's dual impact on campus and community.

> "This state-of-the-art facility will expand learning opportunities for our students while bringing new energy to our local economy." — President Mark Rudin

## World-Class Facility Details

The Spur Expo features two covered arenas designed to support rodeos, equine studies, livestock expos, concerts, and community events. The main arena seats 1,250 spectators for rodeo events and expands to accommodate 3,000 for concerts and large-scale events. Additional facility highlights include:

- A flexible multipurpose hall that converts between classrooms, research labs, and exhibition space for up to 300 people
- Stall capacity for more than 200 horses
- An RV lot supporting multi-day events
- Food processing labs and field labs for agricultural research

The facility was funded through the Capital Construction Assistance Program (CCAP) from the Texas Legislature, with groundbreaking held on March 18, 2024.

## Strong Turnout from Local, State, and A&M System Leaders

The event was well attended by officials at every level of government, reflecting the broad community support behind the project. Texas A&M System leadership was represented by Chancellor Hegar and Vice Chancellor Griffin. Three state representatives — Gary VanDeaver (District 1), Keith Bell (District 4), and Brent Money (District 2) — attended in person, along with representatives from the offices of U.S. Senator Ted Cruz and U.S. Representative Keith Self. Local leaders including Hunt County Judge Bobby Stovall, Hunt County Precinct 4 Commissioner Steven Harrison, Commerce Mayor Stephanie Muller, and East Texas A&M President Emeritus Dr. Keith McFarland rounded out the distinguished guest list.

Dr. Bryan Rank, Dean of the College of Agricultural Sciences and Natural Resources, captured the spirit of the evening:

> "It's impossible to be involved in agriculture and not be an optimist. Everything we do, from raising livestock to planting crops, is future-facing." — Dean Bryan Rank

## Historic First Rodeo

The Spur Expo quickly proved its value when the first collegiate rodeo on March 20-21 sold out with nearly 2,000 fans in attendance. The competition featured more than 10 colleges and universities and approximately 400 competitors from the Southern Region of the National Intercollegiate Rodeo Association.

The Lion Rodeo Team, founded in 2014, has produced a National Champion at the College National Finals Rodeo for four consecutive years. Previously hosting home events at the Hopkins County Regional Civic Center in Sulphur Springs, the team can now compete on campus for the first time.

> "Now we're not going to have to go off campus. That gives more students the opportunity to see what these student-athletes do." — Rodeo Coach Dameon White

## Economic Impact for Commerce

The Spur Expo represents a transformative addition to Commerce's economic landscape. The facility is expected to drive regional growth through job creation, increased tourism from rodeos and concerts, and enhanced community engagement through FFA and 4-H programs. Phase II plans include 12,000 square feet of additional learning space, a USDA federally inspected meat and dairy processing center, and an agriculture mechanics building.

For more information about The Spur Expo, visit the [East Texas A&M Agricultural Center](https://www.etamu.edu/agcenter/).
