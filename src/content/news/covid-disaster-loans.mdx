The U.S. Small Business Administration (SBA) has issued an Economic Injury Disaster Loan (EIDL) declaration for the state of Texas, making low-interest federal disaster loans available to small businesses and nonprofit organizations that are suffering economic harm as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic. This declaration provides a critical lifeline for Commerce-area businesses navigating unprecedented disruption.

EIDL loans can be used to cover a wide range of financial obligations that a business could have met had the disaster not occurred, including payroll, accounts payable, fixed debts, and other operating expenses. The loans offer long-term repayment options at low interest rates, helping businesses maintain stability during the crisis and position themselves for recovery.

The Commerce Economic Development Corporation encourages all eligible small businesses within the city and surrounding area to explore whether an EIDL loan may be appropriate for their situation. The application process is conducted directly through the SBA, and the EDC stands ready to assist local businesses with information and guidance as they navigate the available resources.

Maintaining the health of Commerce's small business community is essential to the city's long-term economic resilience. The EDC remains committed to connecting local businesses with every available tool and resource during this challenging period.
